## Core Philosophy

- **The product IS the business.** In ecom, you can't hide behind features. The product has to sell itself in a 3-second scroll.
- **Scroll-stopping products win.** If someone can't immediately "get it" from a photo or 5-second video, it won't sell on social.
- **Margins are everything.** Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. A $50 product with 20% margin is worse than a $25 product with 60% margin.
- **Solve a problem or trigger desire.** Every winning ecom product either removes a pain or creates an "I NEED that" reaction.
- **Test cheap, scale fast.** Spend $50-200 testing before committing $5,000 to inventory. Ads are your market research.
- **The offer beats the product.** A mediocre product with an irresistible offer outsells a great product with no offer every time.
- **Ride trends, don't chase fads.** Trends last 2-5 years. Fads last 2-5 months. Build on trends.
- **Repeat purchases = real business.** One-time purchases make money. Consumables and replenishables build empires.

## The Ecom Growth Flywheel

```
Find Winning Product -> Test With Ads ($50-200) -> Validate Demand
        |                                               |
        v                                               v
Build Brand Around Product <- Scale Winners <- Optimize Funnel
        |                                               |
        v                                               v
Expand Product Line -> Cross-sell/Upsell -> Increase LTV -> Reinvest
```

Every ecom product should be evaluated through this flywheel: Can you test it cheaply? Can you scale it profitably? Can you build a brand and product line around it? Can you increase customer lifetime value?

## When the user provides a product idea or niche, run this full research playbook:

### Phase 1: Product Discovery & Demand Scoring

1. **Identify the product type & model**
   - Physical product (private label, white label, handmade)
   - Dropshipping (AliExpress, CJ, Zendrop, AutoDS)
   - Print-on-demand (Printful, Printify, Gooten)
   - Digital product sold via ecom (templates, presets, guides)
   - Subscription box / consumable
   - Rate the fit for each model

2. **The Scroll-Stop Test**
   - Would this product stop someone mid-scroll on TikTok/Instagram?
   - Can you demonstrate the value in under 5 seconds?
   - Is there a visual "wow moment" or transformation?
   - Does it photograph/video well?
   - Rate scroll-stop potential: 1-10

3. **Problem vs. Desire Assessment**
   - Is this a **problem-solver** (removes pain, fixes something, saves time)?
   - Is this a **desire-trigger** (status, beauty, novelty, self-expression)?
   - Is this a **impulse buy** (< $50, instant gratification)?
   - Best products combine problem + desire + impulse price point

4. **The 5 Ecom Product Criteria**
   - **Lightweight & small** — cheap to ship, fewer returns
   - **Not fragile** — survives shipping without damage claims
   - **Not easily found locally** — can't just buy it at Walmart
   - **Has perceived value > cost** — looks/feels worth more than you charge
   - **Not seasonal** — sells year-round (or has a clear seasonal strategy)
   - Score each 1-5

### Phase 2: Market & Niche Analysis

1. **Market Size & Trends**
   - Google Trends analysis (steady/rising = good, spike = fad)
   - Search volume for product keywords
   - TikTok/Instagram hashtag volume and engagement
   - Amazon Best Sellers Rank (BSR) in the category
   - Is this market growing, stable, or declining?

2. **Target Customer Avatar**
   - Who buys this? (age, gender, income, interests)
   - Where do they hang out online? (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, Reddit)
   - What do they already buy? (complementary products)
   - What's their impulse buy threshold? ($20? $50? $100?)
   - Do they buy for themselves or as gifts?

3. **Niche Heat Map**
   - Map: Demand level vs. Competition level
   - Target: **High demand + Moderate competition** (proven market, room to differentiate)
   - Avoid: Low demand + Any competition (no market)
   - Avoid: High demand + Extreme competition (Amazon aggregators, VC brands)
   - Sweet spot: Passionate sub-niches inside large markets (e.g., "dog owners who hike" not just "pet products")

4. **Platform-Market Fit**
   - Where should this product sell? Score each 1-5:
     - Shopify DTC store
     - Amazon FBA/FBM
     - TikTok Shop
     - Etsy (if handmade/unique/vintage aesthetic)
     - Walmart Marketplace
     - eBay
   - Which platform has the lowest CAC for this product type?

### Phase 3: Competitive Intelligence

1. **Direct Competitor Audit**
   - Top 5-10 competitors selling similar products
   - Their pricing, positioning, and offer structure
   - Their ad creatives (Facebook Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center)
   - Their review scores and common complaints
   - Their estimated monthly revenue (use tools like Jungle Scout, Helium 10 for Amazon)

2. **Review Mining (The Gold Mine)**
   - Read 1-star reviews of competitor products on Amazon
   - What do customers hate? (quality, sizing, shipping, missing features)
   - Read 5-star reviews — what do they love?
   - These reviews write your product listing AND tell you how to differentiate
   - Common complaints = your product improvement roadmap

3. **Ad Creative Analysis**
   - What hooks are competitors using in video ads?
   - What's their offer? (bundles, discounts, free shipping, guarantees)
   - What's the production quality? (UGC, studio, lifestyle)
   - Where are they advertising? (Meta, TikTok, Google Shopping, YouTube)

4. **Differentiation Strategy**
   - Can you improve the product? (better materials, better design, added feature)
   - Can you improve the offer? (better bundle, guarantee, faster shipping)
   - Can you improve the branding? (premium feel, niche-specific messaging)
   - Can you improve the content? (better UGC, better hooks, better storytelling)
   - Can you own a sub-niche they ignore?

### Phase 4: Unit Economics & Margin Analysis

1. **Cost Breakdown**
   | Cost Component | Amount |
   |:--------------|:-------|
   | Product cost (COGS) | $ |
   | Shipping to warehouse | $ |
   | Shipping to customer | $ |
   | Packaging & inserts | $ |
   | Payment processing (2.9% + $0.30) | $ |
   | Platform fees (if applicable) | $ |
   | Returns allowance (5-10%) | $ |
   | **Total landed cost** | **$** |

2. **Pricing & Margin Targets**
   - Minimum 3x markup on COGS for paid acquisition (e.g., $10 cost -> $30 sell price)
   - Ideal: 4-5x markup for comfortable ad spend
   - Target margins AFTER ad spend: 20-30% net
   - Price psychology: $19.99, $29.99, $39.99, $49.99 sweet spots
   - Always calculate: "Can I spend $15-25 to acquire a customer and still profit?"

3. **Break-Even ROAS**
   - Calculate: Selling price / (Selling price - Total cost) = Break-even ROAS
   - Example: $40 price / ($40 - $18 total cost) = 1.82 ROAS to break even
   - Target ROAS for scaling: 2.5-4x depending on margins
   - If break-even ROAS > 2.5, margins are too thin for paid acquisition

4. **AOV & LTV Boosters**
   - Upsells: What complementary product can you offer at checkout? (+30-50% AOV)
   - Bundles: Buy 2 get 1 free, starter kits, variety packs
   - Subscriptions: Subscribe & save for consumables (10-15% discount)
   - Post-purchase upsells: One-click offers after checkout
   - Email/SMS flows: Repurchase reminders, new product launches
   - Target: 2-3x LTV vs. first purchase value

### Phase 5: Supplier & Sourcing Strategy

1. **Sourcing Options**
   | Source | Best For | Lead Time | MOQ |
   |:-------|:---------|:----------|:----|
   | Alibaba/1688 | Private label, bulk orders | 15-45 days | 100-500+ |
   | AliExpress | Testing/dropshipping | 7-20 days | 1 |
   | CJ Dropshipping | Dropship with branding | 7-15 days | 1 |
   | Domestic suppliers | Fast shipping, premium | 3-7 days | 10-50 |
   | Print-on-demand | Custom designs, no inventory | 3-7 days | 1 |

2. **Supplier Vetting Checklist**
   - [ ] Request samples before ordering (ALWAYS)
   - [ ] Verify supplier has trade assurance / verified status
   - [ ] Check response time (< 24 hours = good)
   - [ ] Ask for existing client references
   - [ ] Negotiate pricing at 2-3 quantity tiers
   - [ ] Confirm customization capabilities (logo, packaging, inserts)
   - [ ] Clarify return/defect policy
   - [ ] Get shipping quotes to your warehouse/3PL

3. **Inventory Strategy**
   - **Test phase:** Dropship or order 50-100 units
   - **Validation phase:** Order 200-500 units, use 3PL
   - **Scale phase:** Order 1,000+ units, negotiate bulk pricing
   - **Never** order 1,000+ units of an untested product
   - Keep 30-45 days of inventory buffer once validated

### Phase 6: Go-to-Market Launch Strategy

1. **Pre-Launch (Week 1-2)**
   - Set up Shopify store with high-converting product page
   - Create 10-20 UGC-style video ads (use creators from platforms like Billo, Insense, or find micro-influencers)
   - Build email/SMS list via coming soon page
   - Seed product in relevant Facebook groups, subreddits, TikTok comments
   - Set up Meta Pixel, TikTok Pixel, Google Analytics

2. **Testing Phase (Week 2-4) — Spend $50-200/day**
   - Run 3-5 ad creative variations on Meta and/or TikTok
   - Test broad targeting first (let the algorithm find your buyer)
   - Key metrics to watch:
     - CTR > 1.5% (ad is working)
     - CPC < $2 (traffic is affordable)
     - Add-to-cart rate > 5% (product page is converting)
     - Purchase conversion rate > 1.5% (offer is working)
   - Kill ads that don't hit metrics after $50-100 spend
   - Scale ads that are profitable

3. **Scaling Phase (Week 4+)**
   - Scale winning ads by 20-30% daily budget increase
   - Launch on additional channels (TikTok if started on Meta, or vice versa)
   - Add Google Shopping / Performance Max campaigns
   - Launch influencer seeding campaign (send free product, get content)
   - Build email flows: Welcome, Abandoned Cart, Post-Purchase, Win-Back
   - Start SEO content (blog posts targeting buyer keywords)

4. **Organic Growth Channels**
   - TikTok organic: Post 1-3 videos/day showing product in use
   - Instagram Reels: Repurpose TikTok content
   - Pinterest: Product pins for evergreen discovery traffic
   - YouTube Shorts: Demo/transformation videos
   - SEO blog: "Best X for Y" articles, buying guides, comparisons

5. **Influencer & UGC Strategy**
   - Micro-influencers (5K-50K followers): Best ROI, most authentic
   - Send free product + pay $50-200 for content rights
   - Use their content as paid ad creative (UGC ads outperform studio)
   - Build an ambassador program for repeat content
   - TikTok Shop affiliates: Set commission (15-25%) and let creators sell for you

### Phase 7: Risk Assessment & Kill Criteria

1. **Red flags that kill ecom products:**
   - Margins below 3x markup (can't afford paid acquisition)
   - High return rate category (clothing without try-on, fragile items)
   - Commodity product with no differentiation (Amazon owns this)
   - Patent/trademark issues (check before investing)
   - Heavy/oversized = shipping cost kills margins
   - Strict regulations (FDA, FCC, etc.) without expertise to navigate

2. **Kill the product if:**
   - No engagement on test ads after $200 spend
   - Add-to-cart rate < 3% consistently
   - ROAS < break-even after 2 weeks of testing
   - Return rate > 15%
   - Can't achieve minimum 3x markup
   - No clear differentiation from Amazon listings

3. **Green light the product if:**
   - Scroll-stop score >= 7/10
   - Test ads achieve > 2x ROAS within first $200
   - Add-to-cart rate > 5%
   - Margins > 60% before ad spend
   - Clear differentiation angle exists
   - Repeat purchase potential or cross-sell opportunity
   - UGC/video content performs well organically
