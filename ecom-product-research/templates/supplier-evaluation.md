## 2. Sample Order Checklist

Before placing any bulk order:

- [ ] Ordered samples from at least 2-3 suppliers
- [ ] Compared product quality side by side
- [ ] Tested durability (drop test, wash test, stress test as applicable)
- [ ] Verified dimensions match listing photos
- [ ] Checked packaging quality and branding options
- [ ] Timed the shipping from order to delivery
- [ ] Confirmed communication quality throughout process
- [ ] Negotiated bulk pricing and payment terms
- [ ] Asked about defect rate and replacement policy
- [ ] Requested references from other buyers

## 3. Shipping & Logistics Decision Tree

```
Are you testing the product? (< 100 units)
├── YES → Dropship or small parcel shipping (ePacket, Yanwen, CJ)
│         Cost: $2-8/unit, Time: 7-20 days
└── NO → Are you scaling? (100+ units)
    ├── Sea freight (cheapest, slowest)
    │   Cost: $0.50-2/unit, Time: 25-45 days
    ├── Air freight (balanced)
    │   Cost: $3-8/unit, Time: 5-10 days
    └── Express (fastest, most expensive)
        Cost: $5-15/unit, Time: 3-5 days
```

## 5. Negotiation Playbook

### What to negotiate (in order of impact):

1. **Unit price at volume** — Always ask "what's the price at 500? 1000? 5000?"
2. **Payment terms** — Start with 30% deposit / 70% before shipping. Work toward NET 30.
3. **Free samples** — After first order, request free samples for new products
4. **Branding/customization** — Logo on product, custom packaging, insert cards
5. **Defect allowance** — Negotiate 2-3% defect replacement at no cost
6. **Exclusive rights** — For your market/country if buying significant volume

### Red flags to watch for:

- Won't send samples or charges excessively for them
- Inconsistent pricing between messages
- No Trade Assurance or factory verification
- Pressure to pay via Western Union or direct bank transfer
- Lead times that keep changing
- Won't share photos/videos of actual factory
