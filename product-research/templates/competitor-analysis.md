## 3. Positioning Strategy

### The 4 Differentiation Angles

Pick your primary angle:

1. **Simpler** — Do 1 job extremely well vs. their 50 features
   - "They do everything. We do [X] perfectly."
   - Best when: competitors are bloated enterprise tools

2. **Cheaper** — Undercut bloated pricing
   - "Same result. 1/10th the price."
   - Best when: competitors charge $50-500/mo for something simple

3. **Faster** — Instant results vs. complex setup
   - "Get [result] in 30 seconds. No setup. No training."
   - Best when: competitors require onboarding, configuration, or learning curves

4. **Niche-specific** — Built for [specific audience] vs. generic
   - "The only [tool] built specifically for [niche]."
   - Best when: competitors serve everyone and therefore no one perfectly

### Positioning Statement Template

```
For [target audience] who [pain point],
[Product Name] is a [category] that [key benefit].
Unlike [main competitor], we [key differentiator].
```

## 4. Review Mining Checklist

Go to competitor review pages (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, App Store, Product Hunt) and extract:

**From 1-2 star reviews (what they hate):**
- [ ] Missing features people beg for
- [ ] UX complaints (too complex, confusing, slow)
- [ ] Pricing complaints (too expensive, hidden fees, forced upgrades)
- [ ] Support complaints (slow response, unhelpful)
- [ ] Reliability issues (bugs, downtime, data loss)

**From 4-5 star reviews (what they love — don't compete here):**
- [ ] Core value proposition that keeps users loyal
- [ ] Features that are "good enough" — no need to rebuild these

**Key question: What do users WISH the competitor did?**
This becomes your feature roadmap.

## 5. Competitive Moat Assessment

How defensible is each competitor?

| Moat Type | Competitor A | Competitor B | Threat to You |
|:----------|:-------------|:-------------|:-------------|
| Network effects | Yes/No | Yes/No | High/Low |
| Data lock-in | Yes/No | Yes/No | High/Low |
| Brand recognition | Yes/No | Yes/No | High/Low |
| VC funding | Yes/No | Yes/No | High/Low |
| Switching costs | High/Low | High/Low | High/Low |

**Rule: If a competitor has strong network effects AND VC funding, do NOT compete head-on. Find a niche they ignore.**

## 6. Speed-to-Copy Risk

How fast could a competitor copy your differentiator?

| Risk Level | Timeframe | Example | Your Response |
|:-----------|:----------|:--------|:-------------|
| High | Days-weeks | UI tweak, simple feature | Move fast, build community loyalty |
| Medium | 1-3 months | Integration, workflow | Build switching costs, own the niche |
| Low | 6+ months | Deep domain expertise, proprietary data | Sustainable advantage |

If your differentiator can be copied in days, your real moat is **speed, niche focus, and customer relationships** — not features.
